## 🌍 Foreign AI Companies (40%)

### Microsoft

**🎮 Xbox AI Assistant Launches Globally** (March 13)  
Microsoft has officially launched its AI assistant on Xbox consoles, following successful deployments on the Xbox mobile app, Windows 11, and Xbox Ally. The assistant integrates directly into the gaming experience, offering contextual help, game recommendations, and voice-controlled navigation.

**💼 Copilot Cowork: AI Workflow Automation** (March 13)  
Microsoft introduced **Copilot Cowork**, a transformative capability inside Microsoft 365 Copilot that moves enterprise AI beyond answering questions to executing real work across workplace applications. Key features:

- Converts user requests into structured execution plans across Outlook, Teams, Excel, and internal files
- Runs tasks in background with checkpoints for human approval
- Uses Microsoft's Work IQ signals to understand workplace context
- Can manage calendar scheduling, prepare meeting briefings, and create presentation decks
- Currently in Research Preview, expanding through Microsoft's Frontier program in March 2026
- Integrating Anthropic's Claude technology as part of multi-model AI architecture

### Meta

**🥑 Avocado AI Model Delayed to May** (March 14)  
Meta has postponed the release of its next major AI model, codenamed **Avocado**, from March to at least May 2026. According to The New York Times, the model's performance currently falls short of rivals like Google. This delay comes after Meta spent billions trying to catch up in the AI race and hired Scale AI's Alexandr Wang to revamp its AI efforts. Avocado will be Meta's first major AI release since Wang's appointment.

**🤖 Acquires Moltbook: AI Agent Social Network** (March 11)  
Meta Platforms agreed to acquire **Moltbook**, an experimental social network designed exclusively for AI agents to communicate with each other. The platform, built using the OpenClaw framework, gained viral attention as automated agents held conversations and coordinated actions across the network.

- Co-founders Matt Schlicht and Ben Parr will join Meta Superintelligence Labs
- Financial terms not disclosed
- Reflects growing industry interest in AI-to-AI communication networks
- Signals Meta's bet on multi-agent ecosystems for future AI systems

**💻 MTIA 300 AI Chip Launched** (March 11)  
Meta launched the **Meta Training and Inference Accelerator (MTIA) 300** chip, designed to train ranking and recommendation systems across Instagram and Facebook. Upcoming MTIA 400, 450, and 500 chips will handle all workloads but focus primarily on generative AI inference through 2027.

### OpenAI

**📊 ChatGPT for Excel with GPT-5.4 Thinking** (March 10)  
OpenAI launched **ChatGPT for Excel**, a new add-in embedding ChatGPT directly inside Microsoft Excel workbooks. The tool allows users to build financial models, analyze data, and update spreadsheets using natural-language prompts.

- Powered by **GPT-5.4 Thinking**, optimized for financial modeling
- Integrates with major financial data providers: Dow Jones Factiva, Moody's, MSCI, S&P Global, LSEG, and FactSet
- Can generate formulas, analyze complex workbooks, trace errors, and explain model assumptions
- Rolling out in beta to ChatGPT Business, Enterprise, Edu, Teachers, Pro, and Plus users in US, Canada, and Australia
- ChatGPT for Google Sheets planned next

### Anthropic

**🏛️ Launches The Anthropic Institute** (March 12)  
Anthropic created **The Anthropic Institute**, a new research organization dedicated to studying how frontier AI systems could reshape economies, governance, and society. Co-founder Jack Clark will lead the institute as Head of Public Benefit.

Research areas include:
- AI safety testing and frontier red teaming
- Labor market disruption analysis
- Legal frameworks for AI systems
- Forecasting future AI capabilities
- Economic transformation studies

**⚖️ Sues U.S. Government Over Military AI Restrictions** (March 10)  
Anthropic filed a federal lawsuit challenging the U.S. government's designation of the company as a **"supply-chain risk"** — a classification that could limit federal agencies from using Claude AI systems. The dispute stems from Anthropic's refusal to allow certain military applications and large-scale surveillance uses of its AI models.

- Filed in U.S. District Court for Northern District of California
- Could become first major legal test of AI governance in the United States
- Outcome may determine whether AI companies can impose limits on defense and national-security applications
- Anthropic argues designation violates First Amendment rights

**📈 Claude Spreadsheet & Slide Deck Upgrades** (March 11)  
Anthropic upgraded Claude's ability to communicate across Excel and PowerPoint, allowing the AI to carry conversations across apps without losing context. Users can now switch between spreadsheet analysis and presentation creation seamlessly.

### Other Notable Foreign AI News

**🗣️ Amazon Alexa Plus Gets "Sassy" Personality** (March 13)

Eligible Prime members receive up to five free direct-message consultations.

**⚔️ Palantir's Maven Smart System Military Demo** (March 14)  
Palantir demonstrated its **Maven Smart System** at AIPCon, showcasing AI-powered military targeting capabilities. Department of War's Chief Digital and AI Officer Cameron Stanley gave a controversial demo showing how anyone or anything can be targeted for military strikes with "Left click, right click, left click." The system functions as an AI-powered Kanban board for military operations.

**📉 BuzzFeed's AI Strategy Fails Spectacularly** (March 14)  
Three years after embracing AI for content generation, BuzzFeed posted a **$57.3 million loss in 2025** and its stock dropped to just $0.70. Despite the failure, CEO Jonah Peretti continues planning to bring "new AI apps" to market. The case serves as a cautionary tale about AI-first content strategies.

**👋 Adobe CEO Steps Down After 18 Years** (March 12)  
Adobe CEO Shantanu Narayen announced his departure after 18 years leading the company. In his farewell message, Narayen emphasized AI's role in creativity: "The next era of creativity is being written right now — shaped by AI, by new workflows and by entirely new forms of expression."

**🖥️ Perplexity Launches "Perplexity Computer"** (March 11)  
Perplexity introduced **Perplexity Computer**, a multi-agent AI system that coordinates multiple frontier AI models (Claude Opus 4.6, Gemini, Grok, ChatGPT 5.2) to automate complex workflows. Each task runs in isolated compute environments with browser, filesystem, and API access. Available to Perplexity Max subscribers.

### China AI Industry Trends

**💾 Chip Exports Surge 73% on AI Demand** (March 10)  
China's integrated circuit exports reached **$43.3 billion** in January-February 2026, a 72.6% increase year-over-year, far outpacing China's overall export growth of 21.8%. The surge underscores Beijing's push for semiconductor self-sufficiency amid Western trade restrictions.

- Export volume rose 13.7% to 52.5 billion units

## 🎯 Free Choice (20%)

### AI Agent Networks: The Next Frontier

The acquisition of Moltbook by Meta and the launch of Perplexity Computer signal a major industry shift: **from single AI assistants to multi-agent ecosystems**. Key developments:

- **AI-to-AI Communication**: Moltbook demonstrated autonomous agents holding conversations and coordinating actions without human intervention
- **Multi-Model Orchestration**: Perplexity Computer coordinates Claude, Gemini, Grok, and ChatGPT simultaneously for complex workflows
- **Agent Social Networks**: Platforms where AI agents post, interact, and collaborate are emerging as new infrastructure layer
- **OpenClaw Framework**: The viral agent framework enabling users to create AI agents that interact through Discord, WhatsApp, and other messaging platforms

**Why It Matters**: This shift from chatbot responses to agent-driven task execution represents the next phase of AI deployment. Instead of asking AI questions, users will delegate entire workflows to networks of specialized agents.

### AI in Healthcare: Amazon's Bold Move

Amazon's Health AI launch represents one of the most significant expansions of AI into healthcare navigation:

- **Personalized Health Insights**: Analyzes medical records, medications, and health history through Health Information Exchange
- **Care Coordination**: Connects patients directly with Amazon One Medical providers via messaging, video, or in-person
- **HIPAA-Compliant**: Operates with multi-agent safety guardrails and clinical oversight
- **Prime Member Benefits**: Up to five free direct-message consultations

**Industry Impact**: As AI systems become first points of contact for health information, traditional healthcare navigation may fundamentally change.

### AI Governance Goes to Court

Anthropic's lawsuit against the U.S. government marks a pivotal moment for AI regulation:

- **First Amendment Questions**: Can AI companies refuse certain government uses without penalty?
- **Supply-Chain Risk Designation**: Originally intended for foreign security threats, now applied to U.S. AI firms
- **Military AI Use Cases**: Tension between national security needs and AI safety safeguards
- **Precedent Setting**: Outcome will influence how both federal agencies and private companies evaluate AI technologies
